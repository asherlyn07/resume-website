BLOOD DONATION AWARNESS



What is Blood Donation ?

Blood Donation involves giving a portion of one pint of one's own blood to meet medical needs. Blood donors undergo safety screening prior to being used to help patients undergoing surgery, trauma patients, and patients with conditions requiring blood transfusions. This altruistic act is crucial to maintaining a safe and adequate blood supply for a variety of medical operations and emergencies, ultimately saving lives.





Why we should raise Awarness for Blood Donation ?

For several reasons, it is imperative that blood donation awareness be increased. Its primary purpose is to educate the public about the continuous need for a sufficient and safe blood supply to support surgeries, medical treatments, and emergency situations. Increasing awareness may encourage more people to give blood frequently, creating a steady and reliable supply of blood. Raising awareness can help dispel misconceptions and worries about blood donation while highlighting the life-saving advantages of the practise. Not to mention, encouraging blood donation inspires people to participate in this simple yet essential act of compassion, which fosters a sense of civic duty.



When is World Blood Donor Day?

June 14th is a global celebration of World Blood Donor Day observed annually. In addition to raising awareness of the importance of blood donation and encouraging more individuals to donate blood in order to save lives, the goals of this day are to thank volunteer blood donors. The globally renowned event is organised by the World Health Organisation (WHO) in collaboration with other governmental bodies and health NGOs.
